# Sources & References

## Ancient Primary Sources

### Poetic Edda (13th century, compiled from earlier oral tradition)

- **Völuspá** (The Prophecy of the Seeress). Verses 50-58. Describes Jörmungandr's emergence at Ragnarök and battle with Thor.
- **Hymiskviða** (The Lay of Hymir). Complete poem detailing Thor's fishing expedition to catch Jörmungandr.
- **Grímnismál** (The Lay of Grímnir). Brief references to the World Serpent.
- **Lokasenna** (Loki's Quarrel). Mentions Loki's monstrous children.
- **Vafþrúðnismál** (The Lay of Vafþrúðnir). References to Thor's doom.

### Prose Edda by Snorri Sturluson (~1220 CE)

- **Gylfaginning** (The Beguiling of Gylfi). Chapters 8, 34, 46-47, 51. Comprehensive account of:
  - Jörmungandr's birth and casting into ocean
  - Growth to world-encircling size
  - Thor's fishing expedition
  - The cat-lifting challenge at Útgarða-Loki's hall
  - Ragnarök prophecy and mutual destruction with Thor
- **Skáldskaparmál** (The Language of Poetry). Chapters 1-2, 17. Additional references and poetic kennings for Jörmungandr.

### Skaldic Poetry

- **Húsdrápa** by Úlfr Uggason (late 10th century). Description of Thor fishing for Jörmungandr depicted on hall panels.
- **Ragnarsdrápa** by Bragi Boddason (9th century). Early reference to Thor and the serpent.
- Various other skaldic verses referencing "belt of the earth" and similar kennings.
